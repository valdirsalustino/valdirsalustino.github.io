Getting Involved in HEP

At some point during my high schools classes, I read a nice paper about the puzzle of solar neutrino disappearance and its fascinating flavour oscillation. Afterwards, I decided to do a bachelor in Physics. Conveniently, during my undergraduate program, I got a scholarship to work at the nuclear neutrino research, project called Angra neutrinos, in Rio de Janeiro. I made contributions in the studies of cosmic ray's background, mainly neutrons flux through the detector at sea level. Check it out.

Master Degree

At the end of my undergraduate, I had decided to focus on phenomenology of Standard Model of Particle Physics (SM). Models with extended symmetry brought my attention because seemed a more complete version of SM, with several new particles, including bi-leptons. You can check my results here.

PhD Degree

During my PhD program at Federal University of Rio de Janeiro I started at the LHCb collaboration, where I am member today. I have made contributions to the Technical Design Report of the Silicon Inner Tracker prototype for the LHCb tracker upgrade program, Calorimeters sub-detectors, Beam-Gas-Vertexing (BGV) experiment and analyses within the Quantum Electroweak and Exotica Physics Analyses Working Group. You can find my PhD thesis here.
